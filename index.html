I made Saxo Bank give money a voice in a category that all sounds the same

Problem

Saxo wanted to grow brand awareness and preference among experienced investors and traders globally. But in a crowded, price-driven category full of bland, bank-like ads, Saxo needed to stand out in a way that felt equally relevant in Asian, European and American markets.

Insight

For investors, money isn’t just something to save, it’s something to activate. And with more tradable instruments than competitors, Saxo gives your money more things to do.

Solution

Dramatise the product benefit of 75,000+ tradable instruments by showing that if your money had a choice, it would choose Saxo.

I helped Burger King UK turn a global strategy into local rebellion

Problem

Burger King had launched a new global brand strategy pivoting Have It Your Way to Be Your Way. But in a market where celebrating diversity was starting to become the norm, the platform risked feeling generic and imported. And the UK had never really understood Burger King's old promise of customisation anyway. The brand needed to land Be Your Way in a way that felt authentically Burger King and distinctly British.

Insight

British youth are constantly managing expectations – from parents, teachers, society. The only time they truly feel like themselves is when they're being cheeky: taking what's expected and giving it their own twist. It's not rebellion, it's spontaneous reinterpretation. A bit like the feeling of deciding to eat fast food.

Solution

Position Burger King as the destination of cheeky detours.
